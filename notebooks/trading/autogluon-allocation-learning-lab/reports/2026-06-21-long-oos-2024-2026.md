## Gate Read

The notebook gate reported `FAIL`.

The overlay beat SPY on CAGR and Sharpe, but it did not clear the exact max-drawdown comparison because the two drawdowns were equal after rounding and differed by only floating-point noise in exact values.

Even if that equality is treated as a pass, this is not a compelling ML win:

- The edge over SPY is extremely small.
- The overlay beat SPY in only 27.5% of rebalance windows.
- Equal weight and risk parity both produced better Sharpe and lower drawdown.
- The latest overlay weights were still basically SPY: about 97.4% SPY, 1.9% TLT, and 0.7% GLD.

## Verdict

This longer OOS run does **not** make a strong case that ML has earned extra complexity.

The SPY-aware overlay is cleaner and safer than the older unconstrained ML allocation because it defaults to SPY and only makes small defensive moves. But as tested here, it behaves mostly like SPY and does not beat the stronger simple allocation baselines.

## Next Research Step

Candidate validation-only improvements:
